The procrastination cycle

This is very true and has been studied by myself

  • Start

    The project is assigned

    In this first stage, a project is assigned that the individual does not want to do. Panic sets in, and all the hours and fun things that this project will take the place of begin to cross the worried students mind. Alternative options begin to form.

  • First attempt

    The beginning

    The student, feeling initial motivation, sits down and begins working. However they do not begin working at all because they instead sat down at their desk and found that BeReal had just gone off. This led them into a spiral of phone usage, only to be inturrupted by the "Battery at 20% notification.

  • Post first attempt

    The realization begins to set in

    Realizing they have just spent all the time they allocated to do the assingment on Instagram, the individual gives up and moves on to somethign else, most often dinner. The process of motivation and ultimate failure due to procrastination is a cycle that continues until the due date approaches.

  • 3 hours before the due time

    The scramble

    3 hours or less before the assingment is due, the student remembers the massive project, and begins to scramble. They remove their phone from the room, knowing how that went last time, and proceed to use ChatGPT and other resources to cut corners and "Just get something in" this proves to be very effective at one thing and ineffective at another. The assingment is in, however it is awful and deserves a bad grade.
